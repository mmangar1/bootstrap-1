The school in which I started my education was also in a refugee camp. We didn't have enough financial help to have all the things that we needed. We had to depend upon the support we got from different agencies. When we arrived in the United States, we got a lot of assistance from different agencies. For the first few months, at the beginning of our resettlement in the United States, my family and I struggled with adjusting to a new country and culture. Going through different challenges we started to learn a new way of life. Eventually, things began to get better.

One of the main problems I faced was adjusting to a new place with a new culture and language. When I started my schooling in ninth grade, I did not have enough resources to get help. I could not speak English fluently and school in g system of education was very different. This was my personal struggle so I started to work very hard, learn new vocabulary, and get help from my friends and teachers who supported me.

We have been able to adjust to our new lives here, but my parents are struggling to look after the educational future of me and my sisters. I am hoping that if I'm provided the chance of getting a scholarship, then I can study for the degree I would like to pursue and go on to get a career that I can take care of my family and make my parents proud.
